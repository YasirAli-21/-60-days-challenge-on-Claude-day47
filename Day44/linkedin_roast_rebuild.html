DAY 4Comment on 5 posts in your target space+

The Value Comment formula: Agree + Add Insight + Ask a Question

  • Agree: One line showing you actually understood the post's point.
  • Add Insight: A specific detail from your own experience (e.g. something you hit while integrating an API, or a RAG concept you're learning) — not generic praise.
  • Ask a Question: Something that invites the original poster to reply — this is what gets you noticed and remembered.
Example: "This matches what I've been running into building backend workflows for AI features at my internship — the integration layer breaks in ways the model layer never warns you about. Curious how you're handling versioning when the underlying model changes — do you re-test the whole pipeline or just the affected endpoints?"
DAY 5Publish Post #2 — lessons-learned / hot take+
Unpopular opinion from someone still early in AI Engineering: the model is the easy part. Everyone's focused on prompt tricks and which LLM is "smarter" this month. But the actual work — the part that takes 80% of the time — is the boring stuff: REST APIs that don't break, workflows that handle failure gracefully, integrations that survive an upstream API changing without warning. That's most of what I do day to day as an AI Backend Engineering Intern. Not the flashy part. The part that makes the flashy part usable. If you're starting out in AI Engineering: learn the backend fundamentals before you chase the newest framework. They'll matter longer.
~700 characters.
DAY 6Engage with comments + 5 more connection requests+
  • Reply to every comment on both posts within a few hours — this is what LinkedIn's algorithm rewards most, not the post itself.
  • Send 5 more connection requests using the Day 3 template, targeting people who engaged with your posts (they already know your name — higher accept rate).
DAY 7Review profile views & connection acceptance rate+

What the numbers mean:

  • Profile views up sharply: Your headline/keywords are working — you're showing up in more searches.
  • Profile views flat, low connection accept rate: Your headline or photo isn't earning the click — revisit Option 1 (more keyword-forward) or check your profile photo/banner.
  • Views up, but no messages from recruiters: Your About section or Experience isn't converting curiosity into contact — sharpen the CTA at the end of your About.
  • Good post engagement, weak connection growth: You're being interesting but not proactive — increase outbound connection requests, don't just wait for inbound.

Adjust one variable at a time next week rather than rewriting everything again — you need to know what's actually moving the needle.
